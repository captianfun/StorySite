Chapter One

Assembly

By Oz Lavenski

This was a big day, the last day before summer break. I was sweating, sitting in a small plastic chair that could fry an egg. It was almost thirty-five celsius.

My name's Mark Brodowski. I go to fourth grade at Croman elementary school, which sadly, had no air conditioning. That's why I was so tired.

My friend June was sitting beside me, watching the whiteboard intently. In my depression, I hadn't bothered to look at it. But what I saw was gold.

On the whiteboard, in cursive, was a message that said 'NASA visit today'.

I'm a space geek. I saw documentaries, movies, and pretty much anything that had to do with space. Interstellar technology was fire in the twenty-third century.

Two men walked in. They had tags that had their jobs written on them, both with 'engineer' in bold letters.

"Hello," said the man on the right. "Before we start, I'd like to announce that the raffle winners for the space collection raffle have been chosen for a special NASA program. The winners are: Mark Brodowski and June Hemphill!"
